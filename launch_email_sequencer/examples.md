# Launch Email Sequencer — Examples

## Example 1: Productivity course for solo founders

**Input:** "$197 6-week productivity course for solo SaaS founders. Tagline: ship the boring features without burning out. List of 4,200 mostly indie devs. Cart opens Mon, closes Friday at midnight. Bonus: 1:1 review session for first 20 buyers. Voice notes: direct, technical, no fluff."

**Output highlights:**
- Email 1 (Day -2): pre-launch tease about "the boring features that kill founders"
- Email 3 (cart open): hard CTA, mentions the 1:1 bonus by name
- Email 5 (urgency): "20 1:1s is real, 14 are claimed, here's what's left"
- Tone: technical, no em dashes, no "I hope this finds you well"

## Example 2: Yoga certification, evergreen launch

**Input:** "$497 yoga teacher certification. List of 2,800 women aged 30-55, mostly current practitioners. Evergreen, no real deadline. Voice: warm, grounded, no hype."

**Output highlights:**
- Sequence_strategy notes evergreen needs *manufactured* but *truthful* urgency (cohort start date)
- Email tone is warm + grounded, not hypey
- abandonment_followup suggests a single 1:1 call invite

## Example 3: Beat-making course, hype audience

**Input:** "$67 'Make a Beat in 60 Min' course for hip-hop producers. List 1,100 from IG. 5-day launch. Bonus: 10 royalty-free sample packs. Voice: high-energy, plenty of slang."

**Output highlights:**
- Email 1 plays in the producer slang
- Email 4 leans social proof (testimonials from IG comments)
- Final 24h email is *short* — high-energy audiences tune out long emails on day 5

## Example 4: B2B agency course, big-ticket

**Input:** "$2,400 agency operator course. 600 list of agency owners. Cart open 7 days. No bonus. Want to feel premium, not bro-y."

**Output highlights:**
- Sequence is 6 emails over 7 days, more measured pacing
- Email 4 is objection-handling (the price), not social proof
- No emojis, no hype words, ends with a 1:1 application call (premium fit)

## Example 5: Thin context, vague brief

**Input:** "Selling my new course next week."

**Output highlights:**
- launch_summary flags how thin the input is
- sequence_strategy hedges and asks the creator to fill in audience + product before sending anything
- Each email body is shorter and more generic
- Notes in the JSON which fields would strengthen the sequence
